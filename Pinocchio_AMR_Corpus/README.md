# Pinocchio AMR Corpus

## Introduction

We present a corpus of Abstract Meaning Representation (AMR) annotations for sentences drawn from Chapter 1 of *The Adventures of Pinocchio* by Carlo Collodi. The corpus covers five languages: English, French, Bulgarian, Hungarian, and Ukrainian. It is intended to support research in multilingual semantic parsing and cross-lingual meaning representation.

This corpus was introduced in:

> Mitov, et al. (2026). AMR Parsing Beyond English: an Experiment on Bulgarian, French, Hungarian and Ukrainian. In *Proceedings of LREC 2026*.

## Sentence Segmentation

Sentences were manually segmented by the corpus creators. In cases where narration accompanies dialogue, the narration is included within its immediately adjacent sentence rather than treated as a standalone unit.

**Examples:**

- `("I love apples.)` `(They are delicious." - he said.)`
- `(Clapping their hands, they cheered: "Amazing!)` `(Great job!")`

## AMR Annotations

All annotations use English PropBank frames, following standard AMR practice, regardless of the source language.

The corpus contains two types of annotations:

- **Gold annotations**: manually produced by native speakers following standard AMR annotation guidelines.
- **Silver annotations**: automatically generated by the AMR parser introduced in the paper.

Bulgarian and Ukrainian are the only languages with both gold and silver annotations. French, Hungarian, and English currently have silver annotations only, which will be added to the repository in a future release.

### Sentence Masking

The original sentences have been masked from the annotation files due to copyright restrictions. However, sentences should remain retraceable from the sentence ID and the annotation content itself. The source texts are publicly accessible — see the Sources section below.
